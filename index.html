What is DeltaHacks?

DeltaHacks is McMaster's annual student hackathon.

We want to encourage students in all fields to come together and build real world applications that create positive change. The difference between DeltaHacks and other hackathons is that we embrace elements of design thinking to make "hacks" have a purpose in whatever your passion maybe in - and have it have a chance of solving a real problem for someone.

The ultimate goal behind this hackathon is to include, not only the engineering and computer science students, but all talented individuals from all backgrounds. We want you to shoot for the stars.

FAQ

    What is DeltaHacks?

    DeltaHacks is McMaster's annual student hackathon. This year, DeltaHacks will be a 500-participant event taking place at Jan 16th-17th. We hope to inspire students to hack for a positive change in their passions - whether that's environment, health, inequality, education, or whatever their passions maybe (and hence the name “delta” - as “delta” stands for change).

    I have never been to a hackathon before, what should I do?

    No worries at all. We will be providing mentors during the hackathon in order to help you learn how to code. You can even read on one of our execs' experience when she started here to see how easy and fun it really is to learn at the hackathon.

    When/where is DeltaHacks happening?

    DeltaHacks will take place on January 16-17, at our beautiful McMaster University in Hamilton, Ontario.

    But I don't have an idea, what should I do?

    Don't worry! We will walk you through how to generate great ideas at the beginning of DeltaHacks.

    How about teams?

    Once the idea generation is done, there will be open mic and networking time to find a team. Everyone accepted will also be invited to the DeltaHacks' Slack prior to the hackathon in order to discuss ideas.

    Who can participate?

    All university students! If you're someone who is willing to learn and wants to take a shot at changing the world, no matter what your field is, then you definitely belong here.

    How much does it cost?

    Nothing! Zero! The event is absolutely free for all participants. We will provide you with the venue, food, Wi-Fi, etc. You just need to show up.

    What should I bring?

    You will definitely need your laptop, phone, their chargers, as well as a valid student or government ID. Feel free to also bring in a sleeping bag, and we definitely recommend some comfortable clothes.

    When is the deadline to apply?

    The deadline for the first round of registration is December 11th at 11:59pm EST, where decisions will be released shortly thereafter. The deadline for the second round of registration is December 24th at 11:59pm EST. Please remember, the earlier you apply, the higher your chance to reserve a seat in DeltaHacks.

    I am not nearby to McMaster, is there travel reimbursement?

    Although we would love to have you, we won't be able to provide individual travel reimbursement this year. We will be however sending buses to certain universities in Ontario (depending on the number of applicants we get from that university), so chances are you will have a spot on that bus.
